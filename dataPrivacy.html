DATA PRIVACY NOTICE

INTERPATH LABORATORIES, INC. understands the importance and sensitivity of your data; hence, we are committed to protecting your personal information and sensitive personal information to fully ensure the confidentiality of health information entrusted to us. Our Privacy Policy ensures that all appropriate standards for personal data privacy and protection are compliant with Republic Act No. 10173 or the Data Privacy Act of 2012 CDPA and its Implementing Rules and Regulations. This Notice embodies how we may collect, use, and disclose your data as well as your rights as owners of this data.

What Do We Collect?

Before and while providing our services, and when you apply for employment, we may ask you to provide us with certain personal information and/or sensitive personal information. This Personal Data may include, but is not limited to, the following:

  • Your personal information such as your name, address, date of birth, sex, contact information, occupation, marital status, citizenship, and religion.
  • Personal information of your companion, relative, next of kin, guardian, or authorized representative, as applicable.
  • Your medical information such as details of your previous illnesses, procedures done (if any), your present illness, current medications, and family history.
  • Pertinent information related to your employment application such as your personal information (as specified above), educational background, employment history, areas of expertise, past medical records, government-issued identification, and tax records.
  • Personal information — any information which, when put singly or together, identifies an individual.
  • Sensitive personal information — pertains to the attributes of an individual that allow one to be profiled or discriminated against.

Why Do We Collect Your Data?

We collect your data for the following purposes:

  • Personal information is collected for identification and verification purposes, assessment and evaluation for job applications as an employee, payroll processing, benefits administration and processing, engagement activities, government benefits administration and reporting, crafting of new benefits, data analysis, sending updates, information campaigns, marketing and promotional materials, and other information related to the services we provide using your personal information.
  • Medical information is collected for health assessment and management, coordination of care, training, research, quality improvement, billing, and payment for services rendered.

Any processing, sharing, or disclosure of your personal data or health information, except as provided by law, requires your consent. You may withdraw your consent (as provided for by the applicable Philippine Privacy Laws) at any time.

How Do We Secure And Protect Your Data?

Interpath Laboratories is aware of the risks involved in the processing of your data; hence, it ensures that appropriate technical, organizational, and physical security measures are in place.

Know Your Data Privacy Rights

  • You have the right to be informed that your data will be, are being, or were collected and processed.
  • You have the right to access or obtain from an organization a copy of any information relating to you that they have on their computer database and/or manual filing system. It should be provided in an easy-to-access format, accompanied by a full explanation in plain language.
  • You have the right to object to the collection and processing of personal information that was unlawfully obtained without your consent.
  • You have the right to suspend, withdraw, or order the blocking, removal, or destruction of your data under circumstances provided by law.
  • You may claim compensation if you suffered damages due to inaccurate, incomplete, outdated, false, unlawfully obtained, or unauthorized use of personal data.
  • You have the right to file a complaint with the National Privacy Commission if you feel that your personal information has been misused, maliciously disclosed, or improperly disposed of, or that any of your data privacy rights have been violated.
  • You have the right to dispute and correct any inaccuracy or error in the data a personal information controller (PIC) holds about you.
  • You have the right to data portability which allows you to obtain and electronically move, copy, or transfer your data securely for further use.
