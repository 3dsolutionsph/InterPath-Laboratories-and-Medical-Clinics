Treadmill Stress

1

Do not smoke within 24 hrs

Do not smoke 24 hours before the exam.

This includes nicotine patches and vape.

→

2

No caffeine within 24 hrs

Do not eat or drink anything with caffeine 24 hours before the exam.

This includes decaffeinated coffee and all types of tea, soda, chocolates.

→

3

Do not eat or drink 3 hours before

Do not eat or drink anything 3 hours before the exam.

You may take medications with a small amount of water.

→

4

Med lists & instructions

Follow your doctor’s instructions and bring a list of all medications, including prescriptions, OTC drugs, and herbal or natural products.

→

5

Wear sports clothes

Wear loose, comfortable clothing.

Walking or exercise shoes are recommended.

→

6

No exercise on test day

Avoid strenuous exercise on the day of the test.

→

7

Don’t take Beta Blockers

Do not take beta-blockers 24 hours before the test.

→

8

Bring Inhaler Medication

If you have asthma, kindly bring your inhaler/medication.

What will happen during the test:

1

You will be asked to undress from the waist up. Women are asked to wear a sports bra and will be given a gown to wear during the test. Your skin Will be prepped. Men Will be shaved appropriately and the skin will be cleaned with alcohol.

2

Ten disposable electrodes will be placed on the chest and ECG leads will be attached.

3

Baseline ECG and blood pressures are obtained. You will begin walking on the treadmill at a slow speed and low incline.

4

Every three minutes the speed and the incline of the treadmill will increase. ECG and blood pressures will be taken throughout the test. You will be asked to rate your exertion levels during the test and report any symptoms.

5

The test will continue until you cannot keep up any longer because Of fatigue or until Other symptoms (chest pain, shortness of breath, lightheadedness, or leg pain) prevent further exercise, or until changes in the ECG indicates a cardiac abnormality.

6

The treadmill will then start to slow down and come to a complete stop.

Instructions for after your exam:

1

After the test you will be monitored until any symptoms disappear and heart rate, blood pressure and ECG's return to normal.

2

ECG electrodes will be removed and the patient will get dressed.

3

Results will be released 3-5 days. You will received an email once results are ready. Make sure that your email address is correct when you were registered at the reception area.

4
